🟢 Bullish Factors — 5 Drivers
① Structural Production Deficit — Output 3.0–3.2 MT vs consumption ~4.5 MT (MoA, GoI). The ~1.3–1.5 MT gap cannot be bridged by domestic supply alone this season.
② Critically Low Buffer Stocks — Ending stocks at a multi-year low of ~4.5 lakh MT. Physical availability tightens sharply through Apr–Jun lean window.
③ Weather-Damaged Crop Quality — Unseasonal rains hit Kalaburagi, Bidar & Latur, Akola (late 2025). Lower FAQ-grade supply → premium for clean stock.
④ Import Cost Floor (USD/INR) — Myanmar & East Africa imports are cost-sensitive. INR depreciation provides a natural price floor for domestic Toor.
⑤ Seasonal Arrival Dip Ahead — Apr–Jun is historically the leanest mandi arrival period. Farmers largely sold; wholesalers ration inventory by raising ask prices.
🔴 Key Risks — 3 Factors
① Govt. Intervention — Top Risk — Stock limits on traders can trigger forced liquidation overnight. Single policy action asymmetrically more powerful than all 5 bullish factors combined. Monitor circulars weekly.
② Record Duty-Free Import Volumes — Duty-free window extended into 2026. Tanzania & Myanmar running at elevated volumes — acting as a hard cap on domestic price rally.
③ Favourable Monsoon 2026 Forecast — Positive Kharif LRF may push traders to pre-emptively liquidate old stock 3–4 months before harvest. Watch June IMD forecast closely.
Watch Points — Next 4 Weeks: GoI stock-limit circular → immediate exit trigger · June IMD monsoon LRF → above-normal = pre-harvest sell-off risk · DGFT Apr–May import data · Weekly arrivals: Gulbarga & Akola mandis
